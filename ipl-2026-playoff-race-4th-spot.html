IPL 2026 Playoffs: The 4th Spot Shootout — What Every Team Needs Today

RR need a win. Punjab Kings need RR to lose. KKR need a miracle NRR swing. All of it plays out today, across two matches and eight hours of cricket that will define IPL 2026's final playoff ticket.

Fine Leg Talk · 📅 May 24, 2026 · ⏱ 7 min read
🏆

Three teams. One playoff spot. Eight hours. If you were designing the perfect final day of the IPL league stage, it might look something like today — high stakes, genuine uncertainty, and at least one side that goes home asking itself what could have been.

The top three have long since packed their bags for the playoffs. Royal Challengers Bengaluru, Gujarat Titans and Sunrisers Hyderabad are all locked in at 18 points from 14 games. What remains is a fascinating, occasionally cruel scramble for the last remaining ticket — and it all comes down to two matches.

Punjab Kings are sitting on 15 points with all 14 games played. Their season is over. They have done what they could. Now they wait and watch.

Rajasthan Royals have 14 points with one game remaining — against Mumbai Indians at 3:30 PM IST. Mumbai are already eliminated with just 8 points. KKR sit on 13 points with their final game against Delhi Capitals at 7:30 PM IST. Delhi are also out of the race.

Rajasthan Royals: Win and You're Through. Lose and You're Done.

It really is that clean for Rajasthan. A win today takes them from 14 to 16 points. Nobody else can reach 16. There is no tiebreaker to navigate, no NRR calculation to stress over, no nail-biting wait for the evening result. Win the match, book the flight to the playoffs.

Lose, and they stay at 14 points — below Punjab Kings' 15. And with KKR also unable to reach more than 15 with a win tonight, Punjab Kings' 15 points would be enough. RR's entire campaign ends at 3:30 this afternoon if they cannot get the job done against an already-eliminated side.

Their recent form tells a complicated story. W-L-L-L-W in their last five matches. Inconsistent with the ball, brittle in the middle order under pressure. But T20 cricket's greatest truth is also its most comforting: form dissolves when everything is on the line. Knockout cricket does not care about the weeks before it.

Punjab Kings: 15 Points Done — But Still Not Completely Safe

Here is the thing about Punjab Kings' position that does not get mentioned enough today: they are not actually safe until Rajasthan Royals lose.

Yes, Punjab Kings have 15 points. Yes, they have finished all their games. But if Rajasthan Royals win this afternoon, Punjab Kings' 15 points are not enough. RR go to 16 and leapfrog them. Punjab Kings finish 5th. Season over, despite a perfectly decent campaign that deserved better.

This is the brutal reality of the IPL points system. You can do everything right across 14 matches and still miss out because one result on the final day does not go your way. Punjab Kings will have one eye on the Jaipur match this afternoon, their heart rate elevated, quietly hoping Mumbai Indians show up.

KKR: The Maths Exist, But the Maths Are Cruel

KKR's qualification scenario requires several things to go right at once. First, Rajasthan Royals need to lose to Mumbai Indians. Second, KKR need to beat Delhi Capitals tonight. If both happen, KKR and Punjab Kings are level on 15 points and it comes down to Net Run Rate.

That is where the dream quietly dies. Punjab Kings' NRR sits at +0.309. KKR's is +0.011. A gap of nearly 0.3 runs per over is enormous to bridge in a single game. KKR would need to win by somewhere in the region of 80-100 runs — or chase a big total with several overs to spare — just to get level with Punjab Kings on NRR. It is mathematically possible. It is not practically likely.

KKR's scenario is essentially this: hope for a slip-up in the first match, then produce a generational batting or bowling performance in the second. Two things that very rarely happen simultaneously on demand.

What to Watch in Both Games

Mumbai Indians vs Rajasthan Royals (3:30 PM IST) is the match that matters today. Mumbai are eliminated and have nothing concrete to play for, but Rohit Sharma's side have shown genuine fight in the back half of this season and will not simply roll over. For RR, the powerplay with the bat and their spinners in the middle overs will be the passages that define the result.

KKR vs Delhi Capitals (7:30 PM IST) only gains its full significance if RR lose the afternoon match. If that happens, watch this game not just for the result but for the margins. Every run and every over matters for NRR. KKR will need to bat out of their skin and win big. Delhi, playing for nothing but pride, could either be the perfect opponent — or a surprisingly stubborn one.

The Verdict: RR's Season in 20 Overs

The cleaner the story, the heavier it sits on one team. And today, that team is Rajasthan Royals. Their entire season — 13 matches, every hard-fought win, every near-miss — comes down to 20 overs against a side that has nothing left to lose. That is either a gift or the most terrifying thing in cricket, depending on how you look at it.

Punjab Kings did their work. Their 15-point total reflects a decent, consistent campaign. If they go out today because of someone else's result, that is genuinely hard to take — but it is also exactly how the IPL is built to operate. Ruthless, unsentimentally fair.

KKR will play tonight, and they should play to win as big as they possibly can regardless of what the scoreboard says in Jaipur. NRR has a habit of mattering when you least expect it.

But the match that defines this IPL season's final shape starts at half past three. Rajasthan Royals against fate. Twenty overs. One ticket left.
