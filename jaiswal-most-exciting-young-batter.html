Why Yashasvi Jaiswal Is the Most Exciting Young Batter in World Cricket

He averaged over 70 in his debut Test year. He plays fast bowling like it's a personal insult. He is 22 years old. Yashasvi Jaiswal might be the most exciting young batter on the planet.

Fine Leg Talk · 📅 Feb 12, 2025 · ⏱ 6 min read
⭐

The story of Yashasvi Jaiswal before cricket is extraordinary enough — a childhood in Mumbai without a settled home, selling fruit to fund his cricket, sleeping in a tent near the ground. The story of Yashasvi Jaiswal in cricket is, if anything, more extraordinary still.

A Debut Year That Defied Logic

In his first 14 Tests, Jaiswal scored 1,478 runs at an average of 71.3. He scored three double hundreds. He played fast bowling on hostile pitches in South Africa, Australia, and England with a confidence that batters twice his age rarely display. These are not numbers that happen. These are numbers that are made.

The Technique

What makes Jaiswal special technically is his ability to play both very late and very early depending on the delivery. Against short-pitched bowling, he gets inside the line and pulls with extraordinary power. Against full bowling, he drives with his head perfectly still, through the off side and through the covers.

His left-handedness creates additional problems for bowlers. The angle it generates against right-arm over the wicket bowlers means deliveries that would be comfortable for a right-hander become targeting problems. Jaiswal uses this geometry instinctively.

The Ceiling

The only limit to projecting Jaiswal's ceiling is that we don't yet know what it is. If his Test debut year is the floor, and he continues developing — adds reliability against quality off-spin, builds his game in difficult Asian conditions as a left-hander — there is no obvious reason why he cannot average above 50 in Tests across a full career. That would put him in rarefied company.
